About

I am a aspiring statistician in Montreal. I earned my master of science degree in statistics from Texas A&M University in College Town and master of arts degree from Concordia University in Montreal. My research in economics focus on game theory. I proposed a solution to handle cyclic preferences exhibited by consumers over different products in my master thesis. My research in statistics focus on application. I developed models to help the Innocence Project predicting the number of wrongly convicted that may need their help. Currently, my foucs is on machine learning, data visualisation, Bayesian statistics and approximate Bayesian methods.
